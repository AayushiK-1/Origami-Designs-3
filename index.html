Chameleons

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are a part of the iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Pandas

  • The giant panda, also known as the panda bear, is a bear native to South Central China
  • It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body
  • Though it belongs to the order Carnivora, the giant panda is a folivore, with bamboo shoots and leaves making up more than 99% of its diet

Camels

  • A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back
  • Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles
  • Camels are working animals especially suited to their desert habitat and are a vital means of transport for passengers and cargo

Pigeons

  • Columbidae is a bird family consisting of pigeons and doves
  • These are stout-bodied birds with short necks, and short slender bills that in some species feature fleshy ceres
  • They primarily feed on seeds, fruits, and plants
